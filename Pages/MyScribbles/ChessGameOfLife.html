Chess: The Game of Life

The lowly pawns
Protect their King
They sacrifice their lives
All for nothing
But their king still stands
With knights, bishops and rooks
Along with his queen,
They are nothing but crooks


The knights attack violently
They jump around not very silently
At last, they get killed
Just like a person billed
The bishops move
They dance and groove
Alas, they get killed by the rooks and the queen
But a lone pawn, marching, could be seen.


The pawn marches forward
It goes through the board upward
Finally, it gains power
The other pieces, in fear, cover.
The pawn, now queen, fights bravely
It and the other side's queen exchange dramatically
But at the end, all that remains is the mastermind himself
One whose power not even the pawns could gain
The King now has all but pain.


But the most interesting thing of all
Is that both sides were played by the same lust
Power
