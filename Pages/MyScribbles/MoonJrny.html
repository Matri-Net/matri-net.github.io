India's Journey To The Moon

The study of the space, the endless abyss beyond, is called astronomy. Astronomers study this infinite void to learn , and one day, explore it. Astronomy has existed for as long as humanity has. From the primitive calendars, to knowing about black holes and neutron stars, we have come a long way.


The soviet union was the first to explore the vast expanse of space in person. The first ever spacecraft, Sputnik-1, and the first person in space were launched by them. But the U.S.A was the first country to land a person on the moon with Apollo-11. They continued exploration of the lunar surface till Apollo-17.


India also set her sights on the moon with Chandrayaan-1, in 2008, and it was a sucess. Chandrayan-2 however, was a catastrophic failure due to a software glitch. But on the 23rd of August, we managed to land on the south pole of the moon. This was celebrated by every citizen of our country. What a marvellous accomplishment by the scientisits at the Indian Space Research Organisation!


The sucess of Chandrayaan-3 inspires us to continue pushing the boundaries of our knowledge, reminding us that our pursuit of the universe's mysteries knows no bounds.
